Today is a day we have been working toward for a long time. BlossomOS Alpha 1 is now available for download, and we are incredibly excited to finally put it in your hands.

## What is BlossomOS?

BlossomOS is a Linux distribution built around a simple idea: your computer should work for you, not against you. It should respect your privacy, stay out of your way, and give you tools that are genuinely useful rather than just bundled to fill a marketing checklist.

We built BlossomOS on top of KDE Plasma and an immutable base, which means your core system files are protected from accidental changes and corruption. Updates are atomic. If something goes wrong, you roll back. That is it. No drama, no lost afternoons trying to recover a broken package manager.

## What is in Alpha 1?

This first release includes:

- A complete desktop built on KDE Plasma with our early custom window chrome and theme
- Arc Software, our app center for Flatpak and AppImage applications
- An immutable system core with atomic updates and instant rollback
- Full disk encryption out of the box
- Early developer tools
- A set of thoughtfully chosen default applications that cover the most common everyday tasks

We want to be transparent: this is an alpha. There will be rough edges. Some things will not work perfectly on every machine. We are sharing it now because we believe real feedback from real users is worth more than another six months of internal testing.

## Why did we build this?

The honest answer is that we were frustrated. Frustrated with operating systems that treat you as a data point rather than a person. Frustrated with forced updates that change your workflow without asking. Frustrated with the assumption that convenience and privacy are mutually exclusive.

BlossomOS is our answer to that frustration. It is built on free and open source software, which means anyone can inspect it, contribute to it, or fork it. There is no hidden telemetry, no advertising platform sitting underneath your desktop, and no subscription required to use the features you actually need.

## What comes next?

Alpha 1 is just the beginning. Alpha 2 is focused on depth over breadth: Arc Software is getting single app pages for AppImages and third-party software, proper install progress tracking, KIO support, and a tighter download manager. The desktop is getting new icons, consistent button animations, and a round of UI fixes across system settings and Qt Quick.

We are building this alongside a community, not ahead of one. If you try BlossomOS and find something broken, tell us. If you have an idea for something that would make it better, share it. The forums, Discord, and Matrix rooms are all open.

Thank you for believing in what we are building. Now go try it.
